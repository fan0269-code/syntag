> Date accessed: 2026-07-13  
> Scope: the four existing topics, two disciplines, six existing fields, and the existing twelve-theory corpus. This pack does not create a new discipline, field, theory, or universal theory-selection rule.  
> Evidence rule: **L1** records are limited to the linked original work, DOI, publisher, university, or authoritative-institution record. **L2** topic/field fit and recommendation labels are Syntag editorial decisions, not facts asserted by the sources. **L3** research-design choices must remain conditional on question, setting, access, ethics, and disciplinary guidance.

## 2. Topic-level theory choices

The labels below are deliberately **L2 editorial**. Each source supports the named lens’s bounded vocabulary; it does **not** prove that a lens is uniquely correct for every instance of the topic.

| Existing topic | Primary candidate | Supporting candidate | Not recommended as primary | Source-bounded rationale and guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Teachers’ professional identity during reform** | Teacher Identity Theory | Teacher Professional Development Theory | Teacher Life History Research | Kelchtermans supports professional self-understanding and Lasky directly studies identity/agency in a reform context. A named professional-growth model can support a separate learning/practice-change question. Life-history research is a narrative research tradition, so it can supply material but not by itself the explanatory identity mechanism. Sources: `kelchtermans-2009-teacher-identity`, `lasky-2005-teacher-identity-agency`, `teacher-development-clarke-hollingsworth-2002`, `teacher-life-history-goodson-1992`. |
| **Educational transitions over time** | Life Course Theory | Teacher Life History Research | Multiple Streams Framework | Elder supports pathways in historical context and across the life span. Goodson supports narrated teacher lives if the study is about interpreted biography. MSF concerns policy agenda/choice, not an individual or relational transition after the policy is set. Sources: `elder-1998-life-course`, `elder-johnson-crosnoe-2003-life-course`, `teacher-life-history-goodson-1992`, `msf-kingdon-2011`. |
| **Organizational routines and structural change** | Structuration Theory | Institutional Theory | Social Capital Theory | Giddens supplies rules/resources and recurrent practice. Meyer–Rowan and DiMaggio–Powell help only where formal structure, legitimacy, decoupling, or field pressure are evidenced. Social capital should not be primary where the question contains no relation-enabled resource-access mechanism. Sources: `struct-giddens-1984`, `institutional-meyer-rowan-1977`, `dimaggio-powell-1983-iron-cage`, `coleman-1988-social-capital`. |
| **Inequality in educational and social fields** | Practice Theory (Bourdieu) | Social Capital Theory | Communities of Practice | Bourdieu supplies the entry’s habitus/field/capital vocabulary. Coleman can support a narrower relation-enabled resource-access mechanism. CoP should not be primary if the required evidence is field position and capital rather than sustained participation, mutual engagement, joint enterprise, and repertoire. Sources: `bourdieu-1977-outline-practice`, `practice-capital-1986`, `coleman-1988-social-capital`, `cop-wenger-1998` (existing corpus source). |

### Topic publication rules

1. Show **primary**, **supporting**, and **not recommended as primary** as editorial recommendations, never as a ranking of truth or quality.
2. State the analysis unit and needed evidence before making the recommendation (for example, professional self-understanding; a transition/pathway; recurrent practice and rules/resources; or field/capital relations).
3. A “not recommended” entry means the stated question lacks that theory’s necessary mechanism or unit. It does not prohibit a bounded secondary use after the question is revised.

## 3. Discipline and field pathways

The two disciplines and six fields are a **Syntag navigation taxonomy (L2)**. No source in this pack establishes them as exhaustive or universally standard academic classifications. The factual content claim should therefore be limited to the scope of the named institutional/source record and the relevant theory vocabulary.

## 4. Evidence gaps and non-claims

1. **No universal field taxonomy.** The available authoritative records support topic and theory vocabulary, not the claim that these six labels are universally recognised, mutually exclusive fields. Publish them as Syntag editorial routes only.
2. **Rural & Remote Education.** This audit found no C7-quality primary source in the existing corpus that licenses rural/remote-specific causal or theory-belonging claims. Do not write such claims until a suitable UNESCO, government, university, or original research source is added and verified.
3. **Discipline-to-theory membership.** A theory can be used across Education and Sociology; the corpus does not support “belongs exclusively to” language. Relations must say what research question and analysis unit make a lens useful.
4. **Topic recommendations.** None of the listed original works proves a recommendation ranking. Primary/supporting/not-recommended labels are editorial L2 judgments and require a visible explanation of the question, evidence, and boundary.
5. **Policy sequence.** MSF should not be used to explain frontline implementation after adoption; Street-Level Bureaucracy should not be used as an agenda-setting lens. This is a scope distinction based on their cited primary records, not a claim that they can never be combined in a carefully specified study.
