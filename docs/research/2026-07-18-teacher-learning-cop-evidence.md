# Teacher learning and Communities of Practice — evidence pack

> Access date: 2026-07-18
> Scope: research support for the proposed Topic slugs `teacher-professional-learning-and-change` and `communities-of-practice-in-teacher-learning`. This is a research and drafting handoff, **not** a publication decision, a corpus change, a scholar profile, or a genealogy proposal.
> Evidence rule: L1 records below establish only the stated bibliographic fact or bounded source vocabulary. L2 is Syntag editorial synthesis. L3 is conditional research guidance. Under [ADR-027](../decisions/ADR-027-evidence-status-and-content-nature.md), the existing corpus records remain `legacy_source_metadata` until a claim has a real locator, reviewer role, review decision, and real `verifiedAt`; this document supplies none of those fields.

## 1. Non-negotiable scope and publication boundary

1. **Teacher Professional Development is an editorial umbrella, not a theory founded by one author.** The existing `teacher-professional-development-theory` record explicitly groups plural teacher-development and professional-learning models. A Topic must name the particular model and proposed mechanism before making a model-specific claim.
2. **A named school, team, workshop, network, platform, or professional-development programme is not thereby a Community of Practice (CoP).** CoP use requires evidence of socially sustained shared practice; mutual engagement, jointly negotiated enterprise, and shared repertoire are the current corpus tests.
3. **No new genealogy follows from this pack.** Shared vocabulary, a citation, a comparison, or an instructional application does not establish an intellectual-historical edge.
4. **Do not turn participation into an effect claim.** The sources assembled here do not establish that a programme, community, or model improves teaching, learner outcomes, equity, retention, innovation, or organisational performance in every setting.
5. **No claim-level approval is implied.** Do not populate `locator`, `reviewerRole`, `reviewDecision`, or `verifiedAt` from this file. No page number, chapter locator, reviewer, or review date was invented or recorded.

## 3. Topic: `teacher-professional-learning-and-change`

### 3.1 Intended question boundary and candidate pathway

**Question boundary (L2):** Use this Topic for a question about how teachers’ learning opportunities, workplace conditions, reflection/enactment, and professional practice are related to a specified change process. It is not a catch-all page for any course, workshop, school-improvement initiative, teacher attitude, or learner outcome.

| Pathway label | Candidate | Status and boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary | Teacher Professional Development editorial umbrella, with a named model selected where needed | L2 editorial route. A manuscript must name the particular model (for example, Clarke and Hollingsworth’s Interconnected Model of Professional Growth) and the proposed mechanism. It must not present the umbrella as one author’s unified theory. |
| Supporting | Communities of Practice | L2 conditional route. Use only when sustained shared practice, participation, and the CoP conditions can be evidenced; it is not interchangeable with a professional-development programme. |
| Not recommended as primary for the stated question | Teacher Identity Theory | L2 routing judgment. It may be a secondary lens if professional self-understanding is the explanatory object, but it does not by itself specify a teacher-learning or practice-change mechanism. |

## 4. Topic: `communities-of-practice-in-teacher-learning`

### 4.1 Intended question boundary and candidate pathway

**Question boundary (L2):** Use this Topic only where the research question concerns teachers’ learning through participation in a socially sustained practice: who can participate, what counts as competent contribution, how shared work is negotiated, and how access, identity, or boundaries are organised. The Topic is not a label for any teacher team or an outcome claim about professional development.

| Pathway label | Candidate | Status and boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary | Communities of Practice | L2 conditional route, not an automatic classification. The unit must be a socially sustained shared practice, and evidence must be able to test mutual engagement, jointly negotiated enterprise, and shared repertoire. |
| Supporting | A named teacher professional-learning/growth model | L2 complementary route where the question also examines learning opportunities, workplace conditions, or practice change beyond participation in one shared practice. Keep the CoP mechanism distinct from the development model. |
| Not recommended as primary for the stated question | Social Capital Theory | L2 routing judgment. Do not make Social Capital primary when the proposed mechanism is shared practice, participation, mutual engagement, joint enterprise, repertoire, or LPP rather than relation-enabled access to a specified resource through ties. It may be a secondary contrast only when a distinct resource-access mechanism is evidenced. |
| Pre-screen, not a theory classification | Teacher Professional Development as a generic programme label | A programme label does not establish participation, shared practice, or CoP membership; use a named learning/change model only after its specific mechanism is stated. |

## 6. Explicit non-claims and stop conditions

Do not approve a future Topic draft if it does any of the following:

- calls Christopher Day, David Clarke, Hilary Hollingsworth, Jean Lave, or Etienne Wenger the sole founder of a single, unified Teacher Professional Development theory;
- calls a professional learning community, department, project team, formal mentoring scheme, training cohort, network, online group, or programme a CoP without evidence of shared practice, mutual engagement, jointly negotiated enterprise, and shared repertoire;
- treats LPP as a required linear move from novice to expert;
- infers changed teaching, learner outcomes, equity, innovation, retention, or organisational benefit from attendance, satisfaction, formal membership, or participation alone;
- hides power, hierarchy, language, boundary, exclusion, or non-participation where these are material to the question;
- uses source metadata as if it supplied a page/chapter locator, reviewer, approval, or real review date;
- creates or implies a genealogy relation; or
- publishes L2 comparison or L3 research guidance as an L1 source fact.

## 7. Publication recommendation and risks

### Recommendation: **draft / research-ready; not publish-ready**

The two Topics have a bounded source register, source-ID reconciliation, analysis-unit requirements, safe English wording, and explicit misuse constraints. They are suitable as **draft research packs** for a future approved Topic-first implementation.

They are **not publish-ready** because:

1. the intended production claim-evidence fields have no real locator, reviewer role, review decision, or verified-at record;
2. Day and Clarke/Hollingsworth are bibliographically checked, but this pack did not obtain full-text claim locators for the substantive model passages;
3. the four core sources do not themselves supply a teacher-specific empirical-effect claim for CoP, so the Topic must remain a conditional theory-selection route rather than an effectiveness page; and
4. any future corpus publication needs a separately authorised implementation, claim-level review, content validation, and published-only route/SEO checks.

### Key risks to preserve at implementation

- **Conceptual overreach:** “professional learning,” “professional development,” “professional learning community,” and “community of practice” are not interchangeable labels.
- **Attribution overreach:** do not turn plural models and adjacent sources into a single-author theory origin story.
- **Evidence overreach:** DOI/publisher metadata supports bibliographic facts; it does not retroactively create locators or review evidence.
- **Design overreach:** L3 materials and units are options, not universal methods or impact tests.
- **Graph overreach:** no genealogy may be added from this Topic research pack.
