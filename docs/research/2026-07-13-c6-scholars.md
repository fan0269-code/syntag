# C6 evidence pack — scholar profiles derived from the existing 12-theory corpus

**Scope and decision date:** Education and Sociology only; reviewed 2026-07-13. This pack is a research and publication decision record, not a biography catalogue. It preserves `src/data/seed-content.ts` as the authored-corpus source of truth.

## 1. Publication rule and audit outcome

### 1.1 Rule applied

A Scholar page may be published or deepened only when the record preserves all of the following without inference:

1. **L1 identity and academic positioning** from a university, publisher, journal, or other authoritative institutional record;
2. **L1 representative work** from an original work, DOI, publisher, journal, or library record; and
3. a **bounded relationship** to one of this corpus's twelve theory entries, supported by that source record. A contributor to a cited work is not thereby a founder of a theory.

Where the page explains differences, influence, or limits across sources, the statement is an **L2 Syntag editorial synthesis**. Research-use suggestions would be **L3** and must remain conditional on setting, ethics, access, and the research question.

### 1.2 Decision

| Group | Result | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Four existing scholars | **Eligible for C6 deepening** | Each has a traceable institutional/publisher profile, a corpus-linked representative work, and a bounded relation to a published theory. |

Adding pages from citations alone would violate the C6 rule. |
| Organisations (UNESCO, OECD) | **Not Scholar candidates** | They are institutional sources, not individual scholar entities. |

This is intentionally conservative. It does not deny the contributors' importance; it records that the required three-part publication evidence has not been assembled for each individual in this phase.

## 4. Required C6 verification rows and wording boundaries

| Scholar-page field | Required level | Evidence and wording rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Name, scholar identity, academic positioning, research field | L1 | Cite the institutional/publisher profile in section 2. Do not add birth, current employment, affiliations, or personal relationships not needed by the source. |
| Representative work and authorship | L1 | Cite the exact DOI, publisher, journal, or authoritative library record. Use the edition and year shown by that record. |
| Relationship to a Syntag theory | L1 + L2 | L1 establishes the source/work. Any statement such as “central contributor,” “integrated formulation,” or “bounded lens” is a clearly editorial L2 relation and must use the theory-page boundaries above. |
| Influence, development, critique, or collaboration | L1 + L2 | Only show a documented sequence of works, a cited reformulation/alternative, or a directly verified coauthored work. Do not turn adjacent theories, citation, or shared vocabulary into a personal relation. |
| What is not attributable | L2 | Place a separate `Attribution boundary` section on every profile. It must identify nearby authors/theories that cannot be collapsed into the scholar's contribution. |
| Research-use wording | L3 | If retained, state that reading and research-design use depends on the study question, data access, ethics, and disciplinary guidance; do not present it as a generally valid method conclusion. |
