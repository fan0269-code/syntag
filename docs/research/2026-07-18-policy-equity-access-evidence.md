# Policy implementation, equity, and educational access — evidence pack

> Date accessed: 2026-07-18
> Scope: proposed source and theory-routing evidence for two **new, unpublished Topic candidates** only: `education-policy-implementation-frontline-discretion` and `access-to-educational-support-and-opportunity`. It creates no theory, scholar, Work, Concept, field, or relation.
> Evidence rule: **L1** is limited to the linked original work, DOI resolver, publisher, university, or authoritative-institution record. **L2** is a Syntag editorial interpretation, comparison, or routing decision. **L3** is conditional research guidance, never a finding, method requirement, or causal guarantee.

## 1. Decision boundary and existing-corpus audit

This pack is deliberately narrow. It reuses the existing authoritative sources and wording boundaries for Street-Level Bureaucracy, Multiple Streams Framework (MSF), Educational Equity, Social Capital, and Communities of Practice (CoP), as established in:

- `docs/research/2026-07-13-c7-topics-disciplines-fields.md` (C7 source register and pathway constraints);
- `docs/research/2026-07-13-c4-d1-theories.md` (Street-Level Bureaucracy and MSF); and
- `docs/research/2026-07-13-c3-practice-social-capital.md` plus `docs/research/2026-07-13-c3-structuration-communities.md` (Social Capital, Bourdieu, and CoP).

The seed corpus already records the following non-negotiable distinctions:

1. **Implementation / frontline enactment is not agenda setting or policy choice.** Street-Level Bureaucracy is for case-level public-service implementation where direct client interaction, consequential discretion, and organisational conditions are evidenced. MSF is for agenda attention, alternatives, policy choice, timing, and coupling. A longitudinal policy study may assign them different stages, but no source here makes either a substitute for the other.
2. **Resource access is not a normative equity judgment.** Social-capital approaches can analyse relation-enabled access to information, support, referrals, recognition, or opportunity. Educational Equity requires a stated population, comparator, dimension, and normative basis; it does not itself identify the mechanism that caused unequal access.
3. **A Community of Practice is not a generic relationship network.** CoP requires evidence of a socially sustained practice, mutual engagement, joint enterprise, and shared repertoire. A relation can enable resource access without constituting a CoP; a CoP can matter for participation or learning without demonstrating that a relation-enabled resource is the central mechanism.
4. **Educational Equity is not a single-founder theory.** UNESCO (2020) is an institutional inclusion/equity source, while Sen (1992) and Fraser (2008) offer distinct normative resources. They do not establish one canonical founder, universal metric, or causal theory of educational inequality.
5. **Status is not claim-level source approval.** Under ADR-027, this research pack cannot make downstream claims `source_verified` or eligible to display “Source verified” without claim-level approval. Under the current content workflow for these new Topic candidates, implementation should remain `draft` until reviewer-approved claim evidence, locators where needed, and a real review date are recorded.

## 4. Topic-to-theory fit matrix

### A. `education-policy-implementation-frontline-discretion`

**Proposed Topic intent (L2):** how an adopted education policy, programme, or entitlement is interpreted and delivered through frontline case-level encounters under identifiable organisational conditions. This is not an agenda-setting Topic and not an outcome-evaluation claim.

| Lens | Fit status | Required question / unit / evidence | Legitimate contribution | Non-fit or prohibited wording |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Street-Level Bureaucracy** | **Primary candidate (L2)** | Public-policy implementation after adoption; frontline worker–client interaction; consequential case-level discretion; documented rules, demands, resources, authority, and routines. | Explains frontline enactment and delivered policy under organisational constraints. | Do not apply solely because staff “serve people”; do not portray discretion as automatically good, bad, free, or deviant. |
| **Multiple Streams Framework** | **Not primary for the delivery question (L2)** | Agenda attention, formulation/choice, dated problems/proposals/political conditions, actors, and a coupling/window event. | Can analyse a distinct preceding agenda/choice stage in a deliberately sequential study. | Do not use MSF as the theory of post-adoption frontline implementation; do not retrospectively assign three streams after an outcome. |
| **Institutional Theory** | Supporting only where separately evidenced (L2) | Organisational fields, institutionalised rules, legitimacy, formal adoption, or decoupling. | Adds organisation-/field-level conditions without replacing the case-level implementation mechanism. | Do not infer legitimacy, isomorphism, or decoupling from a document–practice gap alone. |
| **Structuration Theory** | Supporting only where recursive rules/resources and practice are central (L2) | Recurrent enactment, use/alteration of rules/resources, and evidence across practice episodes. | Can elaborate the rule/resource and recursive-practice dimension. | Do not use it as a generic “structure versus agency” label or make it mandatory alongside Street-Level Bureaucracy. |
| **Educational Equity** | Normative framing only unless the study names a comparator and standard (L2) | Defined population, unequal access/treatment/resource/outcome, comparator, dimension, and normative basis. | Specifies why a delivery difference is evaluated as an equity issue. | Do not treat an observed difference as self-explanatory evidence of inequity or its cause. |
| **Social Capital** | Secondary mechanism only if relations mediate implementation access (L2) | A named relation, resource, mechanism, unit, and access/non-access evidence. | Explains a possible route by which people obtain information, referrals, support, or opportunity during delivery. | Do not let social-capital vocabulary replace the evidence for frontline discretion, policy rules, or organisational constraints. |

**Minimum publication gate (L2):** The Topic should not be published if it lacks a named policy/service, evidence that implementation is already in view, a defined frontline role and client relation, a plausible discretionary decision, and identifiable organisational/resource conditions. If the real question is “Why did an issue reach the agenda?” rename/reroute it to MSF; if it is “Did the policy work?” add an outcome-evaluation design rather than treating either lens as proof of effect.

### B. `access-to-educational-support-and-opportunity`

**Proposed Topic intent (L2):** how access to educational information, support, mentoring, referrals, material assistance, recognition, or opportunities is distributed, enabled, or constrained. The Topic must distinguish the normative question of equity from the mechanism of relation-enabled access.

| Lens | Fit status | Required question / unit / evidence | Legitimate contribution | Non-fit or prohibited wording |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Educational Equity** | **Primary normative lens when an inequity claim is made (L2)** | Population, comparator, access/participation/treatment/resource/recognition/outcome dimension, and stated normative basis. | Makes the evaluative standard explicit and prevents “equity” from becoming a slogan. | Do not call it one theory, one founder, one metric, or a causal explanation without another mechanism and matching design. |
| **Social Capital** | **Primary explanatory candidate when the mechanism is relation-enabled resource access (L2)** | Chosen tradition (Bourdieu, Coleman, Lin, or another stated formulation); relation, resource, mechanism, unit, access/mobilisation/outcome distinction, and non-access/exclusion cases. | Explains how social relations can condition access to resources or opportunities. | Do not equate tie count, trust, network density, or formal membership with resource access, benefit, or equity. |
| **Bourdieu’s Practice Theory** | Alternative / complement when field position, capital conversion, recognition, and stratification are central (L2) | Relationally defined field, positions, capitals, rules of recognition, conversion or failed conversion, and practice/disposition evidence. | Explains why a relation or resource has unequal value in a field. | Do not label an ordinary support network “Bourdieu” without field, capital, recognition, and conversion. |
| **Communities of Practice** | Not a default network explanation; use only for sustained shared practice (L2) | A socially sustained practice with mutual engagement, joint enterprise, shared repertoire, participation/identity trajectory, and boundary evidence. | Explains learning/participation in a practice, including conditions of entry or exclusion. | Do not relabel any network, mentoring programme, online group, staff team, or contact list a CoP. CoP does not alone establish a relation-enabled resource mechanism or equity standard. |
| **Street-Level Bureaucracy** | Supporting mechanism only where access is allocated through frontline public-service discretion (L2) | A public entitlement/service, client interaction, case-level decision, policy delivery, and organisational constraints. | Explains how implemented allocation rules and discretion may affect access. | Do not use for all support relationships, peer networks, or advisory interactions. |
| **Multiple Streams Framework** | Not primary for individual/relational access after policy exists (L2) | Agenda/choice question and pre-delivery policy-process evidence. | Can explain a preceding agenda/choice process if the study explicitly reaches that stage. | Do not use it to explain why one learner received support or opportunity after delivery began. |

**Minimum publication gate (L2):** An equity-oriented version must state *who* is compared, *what* access is assessed, *against which standard*, and *which mechanism is proposed*. A social-capital-oriented version must state *which relation*, *which resource*, *whose access*, *how mobilisation is distinguished from availability*, and *which non-access or exclusion cases could disconfirm a uniformly beneficial story*. If a claimed community is central, demonstrate CoP dimensions independently rather than using its name as a synonym for network.

## 5. Implementation safeguards and wording bank

### 5.1 Safe page labels

| Content level | Required label / form |
| --- | --- |
| L1 | “Source records verify the cited work, institutional report, or limited source-defined vocabulary.” |
| L2 | “Theory routing, comparisons, and the primary/supporting/not-primary labels are Syntag editorial judgments, not universal rules.” |
| L3 | “Research-design suggestions are conditional on the question, policy stage, setting, ethics, access, confidentiality, and disciplinary guidance.” |

### 5.2 Required wording boundaries

- Use **“frontline implementation / delivery”** for Street-Level Bureaucracy and **“agenda setting, formulation, or policy choice”** for MSF. Do not flatten these into “policy theory.”
- Use **“relation-enabled access to a specified resource”** for Social Capital. Do not use “social capital” for a mere relationship, attitude, trust score, or count of contacts.
- Use **“normative comparison of avoidable inequality”** for Educational Equity, and name the dimension and standard. Do not treat equity as a generic positive quality.
- Use **“socially sustained practice”** for CoP only after evidence of mutual engagement, joint enterprise, and shared repertoire. Do not treat CoP and a general network as synonyms.
- Keep UNESCO/OECD reports labelled as **institutional context/policy evidence**, not canonical theories.
- Preserve the Social Capital tradition qualifier on each Work/Theory relation: Bourdieu’s field/conversion formulation, Coleman’s social-structure/action formulation, and Lin’s relation-enabled resource-access formulation are related but non-identical.

### 5.3 Publication stop conditions

Do not approve a later Topic implementation if it:

1. presents Street-Level Bureaucracy as agenda-setting theory or MSF as a frontline-delivery theory;
2. calls every teacher, adviser, platform worker, or staff member a street-level bureaucrat without client interaction, implementation, discretion, and conditions;
3. makes an equity claim without a population, comparator, dimension, and normative basis;
4. calls Educational Equity a theory founded by UNESCO, OECD, Sen, Fraser, or any single scholar;
5. claims that a relationship, trust score, contact count, or network position by itself demonstrates access, benefit, or equity;
6. labels a generic group or relationship network a CoP without practice, mutual engagement, joint enterprise, and shared repertoire;
7. infers causal effects from a policy decision, a social relation, or an observed disparity without a matching causal design and rival explanations; or
8. writes an L2 routing decision or L3 design suggestion as though it were an L1 fact or a universal scholarly consensus.

## 6. Publish/draft recommendation and risks

### Recommendation

- **`education-policy-implementation-frontline-discretion`: recommend `draft`, not public `published`, until its final content contract supplies a concrete policy/service, the implementation stage, a defined frontline/client unit, and source locators for every page-level L1 sentence.** Its theory route is otherwise well bounded: Street-Level Bureaucracy is the primary candidate for post-adoption frontline enactment; MSF belongs to a separately evidenced agenda/choice stage.
- **`access-to-educational-support-and-opportunity`: recommend `draft`, not public `published`, until the Topic declares whether it is principally a normative equity comparison, a relation-enabled resource-access mechanism, or a deliberately divided dual-lens study.** The route must also identify a population, comparator, resource, relation, unit, and treatment of non-access/exclusion. CoP should remain non-primary unless sustained shared practice is itself evidenced.
- **Evidence-pack status: suitable for implementation planning, but not sufficient to label downstream claims “Source verified.”** ADR-027 permits “Source verified” only for claims that are `approved` + `source_verified` + `source_backed_fact`, with locators where applicable, a real review date, and reviewer approval.
